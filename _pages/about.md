My research lies at the intersection of human-computer interaction (HCI) and artificial intelligence (AI). I focus on designing and developing human-centered AI systems that enhance human-AI interaction, improve user experience and technology acceptance, and mitigate potential harms and risks. My key research themes include: Agentic AI, LLM Explainability, LLM-as-a-Judge, Conversational Agents and Interaction, and AI governance and disclosure.

I received my PhD in [Computer Science](https://cs.illinois.edu/) at the [University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign](http://illinois.edu/) co-advised by [Brian P. Bailey](http://orchid.cs.illinois.edu/people/bailey/index.html) and [Karrie Karahalios](https://www.media.mit.edu/people/kkarahal/overview/). My dissertation focused on "[Designing a Conversational Agent for Promoting Participation of Under-Contributing Members in a Small Group Chat Discussion](https://www.ideals.illinois.edu/items/127715)." I earned my Master’s degree in Computer Science from Korea Advanced Institute of Science and Technology (KAIST) and my Bachelor's degree in Computer Science from Korea University (KU).
